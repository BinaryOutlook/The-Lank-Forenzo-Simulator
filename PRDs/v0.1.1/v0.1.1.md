# V0.1.1 PRD

Status: Implemented
Owner: BinaryOutlook
Last updated: 2026-04-09
Iteration type: UI clarity and density pass

## 1. Summary

`V0.1.1` is a focused interface refinement pass for the run screen.

This packet settles two issues visible in the current build:

- Earth mode makes the decision impact pills too dim to read quickly, especially the green and red boxes showing preview deltas such as airline cash.
- The board-packet headline beginning with "Temporary credibility" consumes too much vertical space and pushes the decision tray further below the fold.

This iteration does not change simulation logic, content balance, or theme identity. It improves clarity and screen economy inside the existing run flow.

## 2. Problem Statement

The run screen currently succeeds at mood and theme, but two presentation choices are hurting usability:

- In `Earth`, the positive and negative impact preview boxes in the decision tray blend too heavily into the dark panel treatment. Important choice data is present, but not readable at a glance.
- The board-packet headline is oversized and artificially narrow, causing a four-line block that occupies premium vertical space in the most decision-dense part of the game.

Together, these issues reduce scan speed and make it harder for players to compare options without scrolling.

## 3. Goals

This iteration should:

- make Earth-mode decision impact previews feel obviously readable, bright, and intentional
- compress the board-packet headline so it stops dominating the viewport
- reveal more decision content above the fold on common desktop laptop sizes
- preserve the existing visual identity of both themes instead of flattening them into generic dashboard UI

## 4. Non-Goals

This iteration does not:

- rewrite the board-packet copy
- redesign the full run-screen layout
- rebalance decision outcomes or metric deltas
- introduce a new theme or new typography system
- change Armonk Blue purely to match Earth if no issue exists there

## 6. Requirements

### 6.1 Earth-mode decision visibility

The decision impact preview pills under each choice must become meaningfully more visible in `Earth`.

Requirements:

- Positive and negative pills must be readable at a glance against Earth-mode cards without relying on hover.
- Green and red states must look brighter and more deliberate than the current soft-wash treatment.
- Contrast improvement must apply to the pill background, foreground text, and any border treatment needed for separation.
- The result must still feel native to the Earth palette rather than neon or arcade-like.
- The improvement must not reduce readability when a decision card is hovered or selected.

Implementation guidance:

- Prefer theme-token-driven color changes over hard-coded one-off values inside the component.
- If needed, introduce dedicated decision-preview tokens for Earth instead of overloading global success or danger tokens.
- Preserve the meaning split of positive versus negative impacts; do not collapse both states into the same neutral chip style.

### 6.2 Board-packet headline compression

The headline beginning with "Temporary credibility is still the most valuable asset on the table." must become substantially smaller and less vertically expensive.

Requirements:

- On desktop layouts where the three-column run screen is active, the headline should target a single-line presentation instead of the current four-line block.
- The headline must no longer be the dominant source of vertical height inside the board packet.
- The board packet should remain legible and premium, but it must give priority back to visible decisions lower on the screen.
- Any width restriction that forces unnecessary wrapping should be removed or relaxed.
- Supporting spacing around the title may be tightened if it helps expose more of the decision tray.

Implementation guidance:

- Solve this with typography and layout tuning, not truncation.
- Keep the headline readable and intentional; this should feel like editorial compression, not accidental shrinking.
- If desktop constraints make a single line impossible at every width, prioritize one line on common laptop and desktop sizes and cap wrapping aggressively on smaller supported widths.

## 7. UX Acceptance Criteria

The iteration is successful when all of the following are true:

- In Earth mode, the preview pills in the decision tray are clearly readable in the default run-screen state before any hover interaction.
- Positive and negative pills remain distinguishable from each other and from the card background in Earth mode.
- On a common desktop run-screen viewport, the board-packet headline occupies one line or otherwise clearly behaves as a compressed title rather than a stacked poster headline.
- The vertical reduction in the board packet reveals at least one more decision card, or one materially larger portion of the decision tray, above the fold compared with the April 9 baseline screenshot.
- The run screen still feels like the same product and theme family after the change.

## 8. QA Notes

Check these scenarios before closing the iteration:

- If the board-packet title still feels too large after sizing down, a secondary pass may reduce summary spacing or memo spacing, but only if the headline change alone is insufficient.
