## 1. Summary

`V0.6` is the next major iteration for **The Lank Forenzo Simulator**. It focuses on the least glamorous and most important work after the `V0.5` systems expansion: making more of the authored game reliably reachable, making every ending strategically viable or threatening, and turning balance reports into actionable engineering signals.

The game already has a strong identity, a deterministic local-first simulation loop, a substantial content library, faction memory, an operational substrate, dossier trails, and post-run recap surfaces. The current problem is not raw content volume. The current problem is that scripted and bounded play still leaves too much authored content in low-confidence territory, and several endings do not appear robustly enough to shape player strategy.

`V0.6` should repair that by improving diagnostics, scripted bots, card and event reachability, and ending thresholds. It should not add broad new product systems. The iteration succeeds when future balance reports stop merely saying *what* was missed and start explaining *why* it was missed.

No `v0.6-demo.html` is included in this packet because this iteration has no meaningful visual-design or HTML demo reference. It is a simulation, diagnostics, and tuning pass.

## 2. Product Decision

The stable product thesis remains unchanged:

**The company and the player are not the same entity.**

`V0.6` protects that thesis by making the existing strategic exits and failures more dependable as play spaces:

- `Extraction` should remain powerful, but it must not be the only successful lane that scripted play can exploit.
- `Merger` should reward a disciplined credibility-preservation sequence.
- `Bahamas` should reward early private-wealth and offshore-readiness planning.
- `Forced Removal` and `Prison` should appear often enough in reports to prove that greed, neglect, and exposure can punish the player.
- Low-reachability packs should either become visible under named archetypes or carry an explicit design note explaining why they remain rare.

A useful shorthand for the iteration is:

$$
\text{V0.6 Quality} =
\text{Ending Viability}
\times
\text{Content Reachability}
\times
\text{Diagnostic Clarity}
$$

If any factor stays near zero, the authored game still collapses into too narrow a possibility space.

## 4. Goals

`V0.6` should:

1. Make all five endings reachable in either the default archetype matrix or bounded reachability report.
2. Add actionable threshold reporting so balance failures can be caught in tests or CI-friendly scripts.
3. Increase decision coverage enough that authored packs are being exercised, not merely stored.
4. Explain reachability misses by failure mode: requirement never met, card never offered, card offered but not selected, delayed chain failed to resolve, ending threshold missed, or resource cost blocked the lane.
5. Add low-reachability archetype support for `safetyDenial` and `shadowSubsidiaries`.
6. Preserve deterministic simulation behavior for the same seed, content hash, and selected decision sequence.
7. Keep tuning work grounded in the existing player fantasy instead of adding unrelated content volume.
8. Leave the final `V0.6` report outputs and implementation notes in this PRD's change log.

## 5. Non-Goals

`V0.6` does not:

- implement a new major UI surface by default
- add a backend, accounts, cloud saves, telemetry, or live service behavior
- replace the existing React, TypeScript, Vite, Zustand, Zod, Vitest, or Playwright stack
- rewrite `docs/PRD.md` unless durable product direction changes
- create an HTML demo artifact only to satisfy folder structure
- expand into detailed route planning or dispatcher simulation
- add large volumes of content without proving that existing content can surface
- make all endings equally likely or symmetrical
- remove the danger of extraction as a viable strategy
- loosen deterministic tests just to make balance tuning easier

- Tuning does not make the decision tray feel random, padded, or mechanically generous.

## 7. Workstreams

### 7.1 Report Thresholds and Diagnostics

Add threshold awareness to balance and reachability tooling.

Expected capabilities:

- A threshold summary for default report output.
- A machine-readable threshold block in `--json` output.
- Optional failure behavior for threshold enforcement, such as a CLI flag or a dedicated test helper.
- Coverage grouped by pack, group, tag, ending path, and archetype bot where the content metadata supports it.
- Miss diagnostics that identify whether the blocker was eligibility, tray composition, bot preference, resource affordability, delayed-event timing, or ending thresholds.

The report should move from verdict-only to diagnosis-first.

### 7.2 Archetype Bot Expansion

Keep the existing archetypes and add focused low-reachability lanes.

Required new archetype intent:

- `safety-denial`: prefers safety suppression, regulatory theater, scapegoating, and decisions that risk `Prison` through compounding evidence.
- `shadow-subsidiary`: prefers offshore, shell-company, liquidity-transfer, and concealment choices that build `Bahamas` or exposure pressure.

The exact IDs can follow the existing script naming conventions, but the behavior must be visible in the matrix report.

### 7.3 Ending Viability Tuning

Treat endings as strategic commitments with setup, survival interval, final action, and counter-pressure.

Required review areas:

- `Merger`: ensure the setup card can surface, the delayed chain can resolve, and the final decision can appear before credibility collapses.
- `Bahamas`: ensure offshore readiness and personal wealth have enough mid-game tools without becoming a guaranteed escape.
- `Extraction`: keep viable, but add or preserve enough legal, board, and market risk that it is not the only rational success corridor.
- `Forced Removal`: ensure severe corporate collapse is reachable under reckless or stress-test play.
- `Prison`: ensure legal heat plus safety or dossier exposure can terminate runs under denial-heavy behavior.

### 7.4 Decision and Event Reachability Repair

Use diagnostics before changing content.

Preferred repair order:

1. Fix impossible or overly strict requirements.
2. Fix tray-composer starvation when eligible content never surfaces.
3. Fix bot preferences when content surfaces but scripted play ignores it.
4. Fix delayed-event timing and stale-window behavior.
5. Tune metric impacts, resource costs, and ending thresholds only after the chain is known to execute.
6. Add new content only when an existing lane has no plausible authored bridge.

### 7.5 Regression Coverage

Add or update tests so the repair holds.

Minimum test coverage:

- balance matrix determinism still holds for the same seed and content hash
- threshold helpers identify pass and fail states
- no single terminal ending dominates the tested matrix
- at least one non-extraction successful ending is reachable by scripted play
- at least one automatic failure ending is reachable by scripted or stress play
- `safetyDenial` and `shadowSubsidiaries` no longer disappear from all relevant coverage reports without explanation
- reachability report reaches all five endings at the accepted width and depth, or a targeted test proves the missing ending lane while the default report remains documented

## 8. Acceptance Criteria

`V0.6` implementation is acceptable when all of the following are true:

1. `npm run balance:matrix` prints threshold and low-reachability diagnostics that are specific enough to guide a repair.
2. `npm run reachability:report` reaches all five endings, or the PRD change log documents a temporary exception with the exact blocker and follow-up issue.
3. Default matrix surfaced-decision coverage is at least `55%`.
4. Bounded reachability surfaced-decision and selected-decision coverage are each at least `60%`.
5. Delayed-event coverage is at least `35%` in both the default matrix target set and bounded reachability target set, or any miss has a documented content-family reason.
6. At least one non-extraction successful ending is reachable through scripted archetype play.
7. At least one automatic failure ending is reachable through scripted archetype or named stress play.
8. `safetyDenial` and `shadowSubsidiaries` either clear low-reachability status or have explicit rarity notes with future follow-up criteria.
9. Unit tests cover new threshold, archetype, and reachability assertions.
10. README and any affected reference docs describe updated commands or report semantics.
11. This PRD's build and change log records final baseline-to-target results.
12. Required verification commands pass.

## 10. Implementation Guardrails

- Keep simulation deterministic for a fixed seed and content hash.
- Keep report code runnable outside React.
- Prefer typed content metadata over decision-ID substring matching.
- Do not use random surfacing boosts that cannot be explained by report diagnostics.
- Preserve exit-card availability when requirements are truly met.
- Treat strategic-resource costs as part of reachability; a card that is always unaffordable is effectively dead.
- Update content docs when pack purpose or rarity changes.
- Append a short implementation note to this PRD after each significant balance pass.

## 11. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Tuning makes the game too easy | Track failure endings and terminal-ending dominance, not just success endings. |
| Bots become overfit to exact thresholds | Keep bounded reachability as a second signal and use archetypes as diagnostic probes, not product truth. |
| Coverage improves through noisy tray padding | Require miss diagnostics and preserve strategic tray intent. |
| Low-reachability packs surface but do not matter | Track selected decisions, delayed chains, and ending contribution, not only surfaced cards. |
| Thresholds become brittle in CI | Make report thresholds explicit and document accepted drift when content count changes. |
| Extraction gets nerfed out of the fantasy | Keep extraction viable while adding consequences and alternate lanes. |

## 12. Open Questions

- Should threshold enforcement live inside the report commands, unit tests, or both?
- Should `active` run share get a hard gate in `V0.6`, or remain a diagnostic until max-round policy stabilizes?
- Should low-reachability rarity notes live in this PRD, content metadata, or a generated report artifact?
- Should `safety-denial` and `shadow-subsidiary` become default matrix archetypes, or separate stress archetypes used by a named command option?
- What is the smallest content bridge that can make `Merger` and `Bahamas` reachable without inflating content volume?
