# V0.3 PRD

Status: Proposed
Owner: BinaryOutlook
Last updated: 2026-04-09
Iteration type: Decision-library expansion and tray curation pass

## 1. Summary

`V0.3` is a large-scale expansion of the authored decision tray.

The current build has `21` decisions in a single file. That is enough to establish the game loop, but not enough to sustain the fantasy of a genuinely predatory airline executive playbook. Too many runs keep circling the same labor cuts, debt squeezes, and extraction moves.

This iteration should make the tray feel like a deep catalogue of corporate maneuvers instead of a small starter set. It should expand the authored decision library, reorganize decision content into maintainable packs, and improve tray curation so rounds feel varied, strategically sharp, and historically textured.

## 2. Problem Statement

The current decision system has four visible limitations:

- The authored decision pool is too small. `21` total decisions is not enough to carry repeated runs without obvious repetition.
- Decision authoring still lives in one monolithic file, which makes large-scale expansion harder to review and maintain.
- Tray selection currently reacts to metric pressure, but it does not do enough to protect round-to-round variety once the library grows.
- The present tray underrepresents several airline-stripper playbooks that are central to the tone of the game: merger grooming, creditor trench warfare, asset harvest, shell subsidiaries, outsourced maintenance risk, pension raids, route amputations, and executive self-protection.

As a result, the event system is currently doing too much of the work of creating variety, while the decision tray often feels narrower than the fantasy it is supposed to sell.

## 3. Design Stance

`V0.3` should use airline-industry history as inspiration, not as a literal roster of real people to simulate.

The player should feel echoes of familiar tactics associated with public airline history:

- merger grooming and takeover positioning
- bankruptcy hardball against labor and creditors
- network dismantling dressed up as focus
- fleet monetization and asset stripping
- safety-risk externalization through contractors and paper compliance
- lower-cost shell or subsidiary arbitrage
- executive enrichment and insider-style exits

But the content should remain fictionalized and reusable. The game should not become a documentary or a one-to-one reenactment of named executives.

## 4. Historical Inspiration Notes

The following public-history threads are useful inspiration anchors for `V0.3`:

- Stephen Wolf and Republic/Northwest era consolidation pressure, plus later big-carrier restructuring contexts.
- Joe O'Gorman, AirCal, and Reno Air as examples of carriers becoming acquisition or integration material.
- Frank Lorenzo and related bankruptcy-era labor confrontation as a model for aggressive contract and union hardball.
- Carl Icahn and the TWA/Karabu arrangement as a model for side agreements that continue extracting value after nominal control changes.
- ValuJet-era maintenance scrutiny as a model for outsourced-risk, contractor-sprawl, and regulatory blowback.
- Doug Parker-style consolidation logic, where mergers and scale narratives are treated as strategic products in themselves.
- Carsten Spohr-era multi-brand group structures and lower-cost units as inspiration for shell-carrier and subsidiary labor-arbitrage playbooks.

These are tactic references, not a requirement to build decisions around any one executive biography.

## 5. Goals

This iteration should:

- expand the authored decision library from `21` total decisions to at least `84`
- reorganize decision content into maintainable packs under `content/decisions/`
- add multiple new playbook families so the tray can express more than just generic cuts and extraction
- improve tray curation so rounds feel varied instead of repeatedly surfacing the same few high-score cards
- support multi-step playbook chains using existing requirements, flags, and delayed consequences where possible
- preserve the product's deterministic, local-first simulation architecture and satirical tone

## 6. Non-Goals

This iteration does not:

- add named real-world executives or airlines as literal in-game entities
- attempt a documentary recreation of specific bankruptcies, crashes, or legal cases
- redesign the full run-screen layout
- introduce backend services, live content delivery, or procedural text generation
- add multiple playable airlines, multiplayer, or a campaign map
- solve every balance issue in the simulation

## 8. Requirements

### 8.1 Decision-library scale-up

The tray must stop feeling like a starter deck.

Requirements:

- The current `21`-decision baseline must be treated as insufficient.
- `V0.3` must land with at least `84` total authored decisions.
- Decision content should no longer live as one endlessly growing `core.json` file if pack-based authoring improves maintainability.
- The pack structure must be understandable enough that future agents can keep expanding it without re-learning the whole library from scratch.
- New decision copy should preserve the current tone: sharp titles, concise summaries, and clearly legible impact previews.
- A substantial share of the new decisions should include delayed consequences so the tray remains meaningfully connected to later fallout.

Implementation guidance:

- Prefer a structure similar in spirit to the event packs introduced in `V0.2`.
- If pack metadata is introduced, keep it simple and validation-friendly.
- Avoid adding scale in a way that makes future editing or review harder than the current small-file setup.

### 8.2 Playbook-family coverage

The expansion should cover a broader set of predatory airline tactics.

Requirements:

- `V0.3` should add at least `8` clearly recognizable tactic families or packs.
- Each family should contain enough decisions to feel like a playable sub-library, not a single novelty card.
- The library should include strong coverage for:
  - merger bait and takeover grooming
  - bankruptcy theater and creditor trench warfare
  - labor shock doctrine, pension raids, and scope-clause evasions
  - asset harvest, sale-leaseback abuse, and route liquidation
  - contractor dependence, safety deferral, and paper-compliance games
  - shadow brands, low-cost subsidiaries, and shell-carrier arbitrage
  - market optics, analyst theater, and narrative management
  - executive self-protection, compensation grabs, and exit engineering

Implementation guidance:

- Packs do not need to align one-to-one with the existing `group` enum.
- Use tags and pack identity to create sharper editorial flavor than the current broad group buckets alone provide.
- It is acceptable for one decision to fit multiple histories as long as the tactic is clear and playable.

### 8.3 Historical abstraction guardrails

The game should borrow from history without becoming a checklist of real people.

Requirements:

- Decision content must remain fictionalized.
- Real executive names should not appear as decision titles, summaries, or core gameplay labels.
- Specific tragedies or disasters should not be recreated as lurid spectacle.
- Safety-related decisions should focus on negligence, outsourcing, audit evasion, maintenance pressure, and institutional rot rather than graphic catastrophe.
- The content should read as satirical corporate predation, not trivia about airline scandals.

Implementation guidance:

- Translate historical material into tactic archetypes, not biography cards.
- If a source example feels too specific, abstract the business maneuver and discard the rest.

### 8.4 Tray curation and repetition control

More decisions only matter if the tray surfaces them well.

Requirements:

- The tray should continue to react to run-state pressure, but visible variety must improve.
- Non-exit cards in a round should prefer at least `3` distinct groups or tactic clusters whenever the eligible pool allows it.
- The tray should avoid showing more than `2` non-exit cards from the same group unless the current state makes that unavoidable.
- Exact repeats from the immediately previous round should be suppressed unless the eligible pool is too small to support that rule.
- When a flagged or requirement-based follow-up decision becomes relevant, the curation system should not bury it indefinitely under generic high-score cards.
- Deterministic behavior must remain intact.

Implementation guidance:

- Solve variety with explicit curation rules or repeat penalties, not with non-deterministic randomness.
- Preserve the current special handling for exit decisions unless a better rule is chosen deliberately.

### 8.5 Multi-step decision chains

The new library should support recurring executive playbooks, not just isolated cards.

Requirements:

- `V0.3` should introduce chained decision patterns that unfold across multiple rounds.
- Existing `requirements`, `setsFlags`, and delayed-consequence tools should be reused where practical before inventing new systems.
- The library should support setup, exploitation, and cleanup phases for at least several major playbooks.
- Example chain shapes include:
  - groom merger interest -> hollow out network -> accept rescue
  - float low-cost shell -> shift flying -> absorb labor blowback
  - monetize assets -> inflate quarter -> eat leaseback fallout later
  - freeze obligations -> provoke revolt -> settle selectively

Implementation guidance:

- Chains should feel discoverable, not hidden behind impossible state conditions.
- A chain does not need to be purely linear if branching requirements produce a better result.

### 8.6 Consequence support

The expanded tray cannot feel consequence-light.

Requirements:

- New high-risk decisions should usually have delayed fallout or supporting ambient events.
- Decision packs should be matched with enough event support that major tactic families have recognizable consequences.
- Validation must continue to guarantee that delayed consequence references resolve correctly.

Implementation guidance:

- It is acceptable for some low-intensity cleanup or optics decisions to be mostly immediate.
- The more aggressive the decision family, the stronger the expectation of delayed fallout.

### 8.7 Authoring workflow and validation

Decision scaling should be maintainable by design.

Requirements:

- Decision loading must support multiple files cleanly.
- Validation should continue to catch duplicate IDs and broken delayed-consequence references.
- If packs or new metadata fields are introduced, validation should also verify that the metadata is internally consistent.
- Unit coverage should be updated to check both content floor and curation behavior at a high level.

Implementation guidance:

- Prefer validation rules that help content authors catch mistakes early.
- Do not over-engineer the schema if tags and pack files already provide enough structure.

## 9. UX Acceptance Criteria

The iteration is successful when all of the following are true:

- The decision tray no longer feels constrained to a tiny repeated pool.
- A fresh run surfaces a broader range of tactics within the first few rounds.
- Multiple runs expose clearly different mixes of labor, finance, operations, market, and extraction maneuvers.
- The tray feels curated rather than noisy, with visible strategic contrast between cards in the same round.
- New decisions still feel legible inside the existing run-screen UI in both `Earth` and `Armonk Blue`.
- The decision-content structure is easier to extend than the current single-file baseline.

## 10. QA Notes

Check these scenarios before closing the iteration:

- multiple fresh runs to confirm broader early-round variety
- long runs to confirm repeat suppression and chain surfacing
- rounds where exit decisions are eligible alongside newly added packs
- rounds with heavy metric pressure to confirm curation still responds to the state
- decisions that set flags and unlock follow-up decisions
- content validation after decision-pack restructuring
- delayed fallout coverage for aggressive new decision families
- both themes on the run screen

## 11. Open Questions

- Whether the existing `group` enum is still sufficient for tray readability once the library expands this far should be decided during implementation.
- Whether pack identity should remain authoring-only metadata or appear visibly in the tray as a stronger editorial label should be decided after the first content pass.
- Whether `V0.3` should require the full `84+` decision landing in one implementation batch or permit a staged ship with the structure plus a smaller first wave should be resolved before coding starts.
