# Highwire Theme Reference

Highwire is the dark, high-tension counterpart to Earth. It should feel like an operating room for corporate brinkmanship: graphite surfaces, cyan signal language, restrained yellow warning pressure, and almost no decorative warmth.

## Direction

- Surfaces: dark graphite and deep blue-green black, separated by crisp borders and modest elevation.
- Primary accent: electric cyan, used as a signal color rather than a decorative wash.
- Secondary pressure: acid yellow only for warning emphasis and atmospheric tension.
- Status colors: success stays green, danger stays red, and both remain readable on dark preview panels.
- Mood: precise, compressed, and alert without becoming neon arcade styling.

## Guardrails

- Do not drift into a blue-only enterprise palette; cyan must sit against graphite, not navy.
- Keep shadows functional and restrained.
- Avoid broad gradients, purple casts, or warm brown/orange dominance.
